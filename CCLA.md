# Corporate Contributor License Agreement (CCLA)

If you (or your employees) contribute to **bino-cli** in the course of employment, your employer must sign this Corporate Contributor License Agreement in addition to each contributing individual signing the [ICLA](CLA.md).

This CCLA is adapted from the [Apache Software Foundation CCLA v2.0](https://www.apache.org/licenses/cla-corporate.pdf). The full canonical text applies, with the following project-specific substitutions:

| Apache CCLA placeholder     | bino-cli substitution                                                                             |
| --------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "the Foundation" / "ASF"    | **Sven Herrmann** (the "Project Owner")                                                           |
| "the Foundation's projects" | **the bino-cli project and any successor or derivative projects maintained by the Project Owner** |
| Contact address             | **sven@bino.bi**                                                                                  |

## Summary of what your company grants

Identical grants to the [ICLA](CLA.md) — copyright license, patent license, and representations — but made by the Corporation on behalf of all employees listed in Schedule A of the signed CCLA. The grants **permit the Project Owner to relicense Contributions under any terms, including proprietary/commercial terms**.

## How to sign

1. Download the [Apache CCLA v2.0](https://www.apache.org/licenses/cla-corporate.pdf) as the canonical text.
2. In Schedule A, list the GitHub usernames (and legal names) of all employees authorized to contribute to bino-cli.
3. An officer of the Corporation with signing authority signs the document.
4. Email the signed PDF to **sven@bino.bi**.
5. Once countersigned, the Project Owner will add the listed employees to the CLA Assistant allow-list for this repository.

Each listed employee must still sign the [ICLA](CLA.md) via the CLA Assistant bot on their first pull request.

## Updating Schedule A

To add or remove employees, email an updated Schedule A signed by a company officer to **sven@bino.bi**.
